Developing a Canadian
Antimicrobial Resistance (AMR) Network

Antimicrobials, used to treat infections in humans and animals, are losing their effectiveness. The implications are stark. Like other countries, Canada must take action to slow the rising trend of AMR.

But we need more than a government response.

We are pleased to share that the Public Health Agency of Canada (PHAC)-funded AMR Network project is officially under way. This project will propose models of what a Canadian AMR network - a unifying network to bridge the diverse One Health stakeholders in this area - would look like. We have one year to design, refine, and recommend models

We invite you to be part of this important work.

Collaboration

Building on AMR work already under way, we will collaborate with interested parties from coast to coast to coast to develop a model(s) for a Network that will lead in tackling AMR using a One Health (human, animal, environment) approach. The Network will identify the gaps and issues that need to be most urgently addressed, leverage ongoing efforts and innovative practices, coordinate and accelerate actions, and communicate opportunities and sustainable solutions.

Action plan

The World Health Organization (WHO) set wheels in motion with a Global Action Plan. The Pan-Canadian AMR Framework is the first phase in Canada’s response to AMR and provides a foundation to identify and develop steps to address AMR and AMU challenges. The second phase focuses on the development of a corresponding pan-Canadian action plan. This action plan will further define roles and responsibilities of F/P/T governments, lay out the details of concrete deliverables, timelines, measurable outcomes, priorities and allow for the tracking of progress against actions.

The proposed purpose of a pan-Canadian AMR Network, defined through an initial consultation with AMR experts, is to undertake the following actions:

01Leadership

Provide leadership by creating an ongoing and inclusive forum that discusses, advises, and prioritizes pan-Canadian AMR activities under the One Health model

  • Identify obstacles that interfere with progress, e.g. policy or structural issues
  • Identify opportunities to connect One Health partners to bring solutions to scale

02Facilitate

Facilitate efforts to deliver on the goals of the pan-Canadian Federal/Provincial/Territorial detailed AMR Action Plan. The Action Plan pillars (based on the Pan-Canadian Framework on AMR) are: Research & Innovation, Surveillance, Antimicrobial Stewardship, and Infection Prevention and Control

03Build Trust

  • Be the trusted voice of AMR in Canada by creating consistent and responsive messaging by stakeholders
  • Increase awareness of AMR in Canada
  • Coordinate and engagement with international community and funders

We are just getting started and want to engage with a wide range of stakeholders, including known experts, key organizations, and emerging stakeholders.

We are in the initial phases of the project and invite you to join us - either by staying updated on the project through the updates that will be posted on this website, or by taking it a step further and sharing your contact information so we can reach out to you directly.
